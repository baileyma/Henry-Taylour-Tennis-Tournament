History

The Henry Taylour Tennis Tournament was founded in 2009 by Lord Henry James Anthony Christopher Taylour, an Oxfordshire-born businessman, folk musician and tennis player.
Taylour hosted the tournament's first renewal at Shipton Manor around September 2009

Past winners

The winner of the tournament's inaugural competition was Gloucestershire-born commercial real estate debt broker Luke Cheetham. During the 2010s, software developer Matt Bailey won several tournaments and was long the institution's most successful player. However, Trinidadian drinks salesman Henry Mills, who rose to prominence in the late 2010s and early 2020s, is currently in the lead with eight tournaments
